We were traditionally a Catholic country. This time (1983) when I was born and grew up, the internet did not exist in Venezuela well at least it was no accessible and undoubtedly access to oriental literature in a physical way was a bit difficult, especially for low-income people.

Crisis, corruption, drug trafficking was the "daily bread" in Venezuelan society, even in the pre-Ch√°vez era. It would be unimaginable that in such a Western and Christian country, Eastern ideological currents could arise, as well as Gnostic religions with a profound Buddhist influence, it was curious how the Venezuelan idiosyncrasy culturalized the Buddhist statues "brought by Chinese immigrants" into objects of adornment and good luck.I never imagined at that time, the philosophy hidden behind the fat smiling figures,but...

In the late 80s and early 90s Japanese oriental animated content was broadcast on television, this anime caused an incredible boom in the youth of that time, many of these dramas contained oriental philosophies that were undermining the adolescent subconscious mind, we have the case of "Saint Seya" where we can see the intrinsic Buddhist philosophy in many characters. as well as, we can consider that the birth of television programs based on yoga or mindfulness content obviously influenced by American television channels catapulted the popularization of oriental philosophies

In this case, we could not be sure that Buddhism entered on television, but surely the deterioration of the reputation of the Catholic Church allowed the popularization of new philosophical and religious currents in Venezuela, such as Buddhism.

How Of Meditation A Day Changed My Life

In my personal case when I meditated for the first time, I never read about meditation or had any concept of Buddhism at that moment,in my mind I really felt identified with American culture "grunge, basketball, video games, clothes and things like that" I was a common teenager 16-year-old with a very heavy emotional burden, my parents had divorced due to domestic abuse, our economic situation was worse it was really hard to buy food, my mother worked as a street vendor but she didn't earn much money, she was a Colombian immigrant in Venezuela, and I had dropped out of high school.

It was definitely a very difficult time, I really felt that I was drowning in anxiety, loneliness, lack of courage, frustration, resentment, fear, lack of support, multiple headaches, until one night I was very tired of everything, I just closed my eyes, I felt my heart and began a battle against my mind, where my mind reproached me for everything like an enemy, but I just ignored it, I remember praying to God with great force "Even though I was an atheist" and trying of identifying myself with the light that sprouted in my, when suddenly my mind became silent, my being expanded as if it were in another dimension and all my frustrations disappeared, the light wrapped me up and I understood that my internal voice was not necessary.

other indescribable things happened which would be impossible to tell here. It really changed my way of seeing the world, after that experience I began to be interested in metaphysics, occultism and things like gnostic religions "really waste of time" eventually I was consumed by the world, as it was supposed to be, until a few years ago I returned to meditate again, which I highly recommend for people who, like me, do not identify with any type of religious belief, but feel our integration with the universe is necessary.
